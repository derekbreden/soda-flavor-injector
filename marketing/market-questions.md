# Competitive Market Questions

*Compiled April 6, 2026. Detailed research for each company is in `/marketing/companies/`.*

## Question 1: How likely are they to produce a home soda machine that competes with ours in the next 10 years? (1-10)

| Company | Score | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| **Aarke** | **1** | Zero patents, zero hiring, zero signals in inline carbonation, refrigeration, plumbing, firmware, or flavor injection. PE-owned (Altor, approaching exit window). Losing money (-2.6% margin). Carbonator Pro enjoined from sale in Europe by SodaStream patent case. 45 employees. Their most complex product is a drip coffee maker that had reliability issues. They are not building toward this. |
| **SodaStream** | **3** | PepsiCo has the money, flavor portfolio, and even a commercial plumbed-in product (SodaStream Professional). But it would cannibalize their profitable CO2 refill stream and core can business, require installation-based distribution incompatible with retail shelves, and demand a price point far above their range. Goodwill impairment charges signal PepsiCo is managing SodaStream costs, not investing in radical new categories. |
| **Ninja Thirsti** | **3** | SharkNinja has real engineering ($384M/year R&D, 1,300 engineers) and the Thirsti proves they're interested in the space. But a plumbed-in system is antithetical to their plug-and-play, big-box retail, consumable-lock-in business model. More likely they iterate Thirsti (add chiller, get brand-name licenses) — making a better countertop gadget, not a tap. Capability is real, strategic mismatch is severe. |
| **PepsiCo (cans)** | **4** | Has every asset needed: SodaStream, brand-name syrup, unlimited capital. But the Innovator's Dilemma is the core issue — every home dispense cannibalizes a high-margin can sale. SodaStream track record is damning ($3.2B acquisition, $1B+ write-down). Organizational DNA is CPG, not consumer appliances. The moderate threat is not that they build a competitor, but that they acquire one if the category proves viable. |
| **Coca-Cola (cans)** | **2** | Has Freestyle micro-dosing technology (50K+ commercial units) and the brands, but faces crippling structural barriers: independent bottler network would revolt at home concentrate sales, Keurig Kold disaster ($1.25B investment, discontinued in 10 months), and Coca-Cola flavors are NOT available as home-consumer concentrate. North America operating income grew 11% in 2025 — no competitive urgency. |

## Question 3: What do people love, and what do people hate?

| Company | Love | Hate |
|---|---|---|
| **Aarke** | Beautiful design object that makes people proud to leave on their counter — the only soda maker treated as a kitchen showpiece. | O-ring failures at 12-18 months of daily use, and customer service that ghosts you after the 2-year warranty expires. |
| **SodaStream** | Cheap, sustainable plain sparkling water at home that genuinely replaces LaCroix and Perrier, with easy Quick Connect CO2 swap. | Water goes flat within hours of bottling, flavors taste nothing like real soda, and CO2 refill logistics are expensive and annoying — most buyers shelve the machine within months. |
| **Ninja Thirsti** | Single-serve convenience with integrated flavor delivery — put a glass under the nozzle, press a button, get a customized sparkling drink without the mess of bottles and manual syrup dosing. | Generic artificial-tasting flavor pods (sucralose aftertaste, "soapy" flavors) and expensive locked-down CO2 canisters ($30 vs SodaStream's $15). |
| **PepsiCo (cans)** | Zero thought required — open fridge, grab, pop, drink, same perfect product every time, anywhere. | Hauling cases from the store every week, 730 empty cans per household per year accumulating in the recycling bin, and the moment you open the fridge to find the shelf empty. |
| **Coca-Cola (cans)** | Perfectly consistent, universally available, the most frictionless way to drink soda that exists. | Getting it home is a chore — 10-pound 12-packs hauled weekly, 624 cans per year to rinse and recycle, and the moment the fridge is empty and the store is the only option. |
