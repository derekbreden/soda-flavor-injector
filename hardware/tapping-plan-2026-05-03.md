# Tapping plan — Snapshot 2026-05-03

**This is a point-in-time snapshot, not a living document.** Captures the plan committed to on the eve of the first 1/4-18 NPT tap into a 316L endcap plate. The Amazon tooling batch (WEN drill press, Brown & Sharpe tap guide, Drill America tap + wrench, 4-7/8" hole saw, Baltic birch, MDF, C-clamps) was delivered Saturday 2026-05-02 and is sitting unpacked. The single goal for Sunday morning is one successful tap in one plate.

## TL;DR — the six-step plan

The user's own distillation, in their own words:

> - Mount press
> - Set belt speed
> - Cut birch pocket
> - Disc in pocket
> - Load tap guide
> - Wrench the tap

That's the morning. No production cadence. No fixture refinement. One hole.

## Each step, expanded

The expansion below is supporting detail behind the six bullets above — not a separate plan.

### Mount press
C-clamp the WEN 4208T base to one of the VEVOR benches at two opposite corners. Plug in, spin test.

### Set belt speed
Open the cover, drop the belt to slowest speed (740 RPM, smallest motor pulley → largest spindle pulley). This is for the hole-saw cut. Tapping is hand-driven, so the speed setting doesn't matter after this.

### Cut birch pocket
Stack on the table: 1/4" MDF (sacrificial) under, one 12mm Baltic birch piece on top. Mark the Baltic birch center. The 8"×8" stock overhangs the 6.5" table on all sides — two C-clamps grip the overhang down to the table edges. Plunge slow with the 4-7/8" hole saw, retract every 1/4" to clear the chip plug. If the stack walks, the clamps weren't tight; redo them. Drop a 316 disc in. Should sit flush with ~0.015" play. Snug-but-seats is better than loose.

### Disc in pocket
Pull the chewed-up MDF, slide a fresh 1/4" MDF sheet under the pocket piece (the tap will bury into it on breakthrough — that's its job). Disc in pocket. Slide the whole stack until pilot hole #1 is directly under the chuck. Two C-clamps lock the stack to the table.

### Load tap guide
Chuck the Brown & Sharpe spring tap guide. Tap Magic generously into the pilot hole and onto the first inch of the tap. Engage the tap in the pilot. Lower the chuck until the guide tip seats in the tap's center divot, spring compressed ~1/4". Lock the depth or just hold it.

### Wrench the tap
Drill America wrench on the tap. CW, slow. Half turn forward, quarter back to break the chip. Re-fluid every few turns. It gets harder as the taper bites deeper — that's correct, not a problem. Stop after 4–5 full threads of engagement (you'll feel it transition out of the free-cutting phase). Back out clean. Hand-thread any 1/4 NPT fitting in — should snug firm with 2–3 threads showing. Done.
