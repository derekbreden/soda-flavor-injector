- The faucet got its outer shell
  - Before today, the shell that wraps the faucet body did not exist yet
  - After today, it has:
    - A round mounting plate with holes for the threaded shank and the two flavor tubes
    - A fitted shroud that wraps the faucet body from the deck up to where the lever lives
    - Pockets carved out so the lever has room to swing both up and down without scraping
- The carbonator's foam shell got tougher
  - Before today, walls were thin enough to warp during printing, the slicer was throwing errors about a support wedge inside, and the snap-in copper plugs were a loose fit
  - After today, it has:
    - Two-millimeter walls everywhere instead of one, so the print holds its shape
    - A redesigned support ring that no longer trips the slicer
    - Clip-on rails on the copper plugs so they grab the wall and stay put
    - A wider gap for a quarter-inch copper coil so the real tubing actually fits
